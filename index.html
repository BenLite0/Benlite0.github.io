On the topic of Dropping

"The supreme art of war is to stump the enemy without stalling." -Sun Tzu

It is possible to lose a double roll due to dropping. Briefly explained this is because dropping a log causes you to interact with the tree a tick later than required for a stall. *For more indepth explination see below.

Below is 2 examples of dropping, 1 that gives double rolls and 1 that only gives single rolls:

In this example I am dropping a log and interacting with the tree on tick 3 giving only 1 roll.

In this next example I am dropping a log on tick 1, the tick my 3t action starts and I move. I also drop a log on tick 2 the tick after I move but I'm sure to also red click the tree to start my interaction and stall on tick 2. Notice on tick 3 I do nothing but let the stall finish.

*In order for a stall to happen you must interact with the object(tree) the tick after you move. The loss of a roll isnt specifically because of a log dropping but more because your not interacting with the tree on tick 2 and/or clearing your interaction on the tree from tick 2.
